Project Scope

Literature Survey

The JOB VISTA project leverages AI-driven technologies to revolutionize career guidance in the computing industry through intelligent personality profiling and adaptive learning. Recent studies highlight the effectiveness of integrating Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) to enhance learning personalization and improve recruitment outcomes. For instance, research by Sharma et al. explores how adaptive quizzes and learning classification can improve skill development, while Kumar and Lee demonstrate the value of AI-generated career suggestions based on user behavior and learning pace.

Instead of traditional deep learning models job vista using transformer-based BERT classifier & Random Forest Classifier have enabled more accurate personality predictions using both self-assessments and employer feedback. Tools like Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) enhance quiz generation and real-time content delivery, while affective computing techniques support confidence analysis during mock interviews. JOB VISTA integrates these innovations into a unified platform, offering dynamic CV creation, emotionally intelligent interview guidance, and career recommendations tailored to individual strengths, goals, and evolving skillsets. Research Gap

Job Vista project addresses the following research gaps:

Fragmented Career Development Systems

While tools for personality profiling, adaptive learning, and job guidance exist, they function in isolation. Users face disconnected experiences, where personality insights, learning progress, and career suggestions don’t interact—resulting in generic outcomes that fail to support holistic professional growth.

Lack of Emotionally Adaptive Learning

Current adaptive learning platforms focus only on quiz performance, ignoring emotional cues like frustration, boredom, or overload. Without emotional awareness, these systems miss the opportunity to adjust learning content in real time, leading to reduced engagement and poor knowledge retention.

Static and Impersonal Career Guidance

Most career platforms use fixed algorithms based on skills or interests, overlooking psychological traits and evolving user goals. This results in job recommendations that don’t align with users' true potential or emotional readiness—especially for tech fields like web development that demand agility and continuous learning.

Underutilization of Advanced AI & Real-Time Feedback

Emerging technologies like generative AI, LLMs, and real-time emotion tracking are underused in career systems. A unified platform using BERT classifiers and Random Forest models can enable personalized guidance, emotional analysis, adaptive learning, and career recommendations based on personality, behavior, and performance.

Limited Personalization in Interview Preparation

Most mock interview systems provide generic questions without adapting to a candidate’s emotional state, skill level, or previous performance. This limits the ability to prepare users effectively for high-stakes interviews, especially in computing careers that require both technical expertise and soft skills.

Insufficient Data-Driven Career Progress Tracking

Existing platforms lack robust analytics to track users' long-term career development. Without integrating behavior patterns, feedback trends, and evolving goals, systems fail to offer proactive suggestions or identify when users are off-track, reducing overall career success potential.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can an AI-driven system enhance career development in computing by integrating personality profiling, adaptive learning, and emotionally intelligent feedback?

Despite advancements in AI and educational technologies, current career development platforms remain fragmented and one-dimensional. Users often encounter disconnected systems for learning, personality assessment, and job matching, which results in generic recommendations and poor alignment with their actual strengths and goals. Additionally, emotional readiness and behavioral traits are typically overlooked, leading to mismatches in job placements and reduced long-term satisfaction. This is especially problematic in dynamic fields like computing, where continuous learning, adaptability, and psychological compatibility are essential for success.


Job Vista Demonstration - Solution Overview

Proposed Solution

JOB VISTA introduces an AI-powered career development platform specifically designed for the computing sector. The system addresses key challenges through:

  • Dual Personality Profiling using BERT and Random Forest Classifier to combine self-assessment with employer feedback
  • Adaptive Learning Pathways based on real-time performance, learning speed, and quiz outcomes
  • Emotionally Intelligent Feedback using affective computing to assess confidence and emotional state during mock interviews
  • Dynamic CV Generation aligned with a user’s skills, learning progress, and behavioral insights
  • Personalized Job Matching through NLP and vector-based algorithms that analyze both technical and psychological traits
  • Real-Time Quiz Adjustment & Content Delivery using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for tailored learning experiences
By combining AI, behavioral analytics, and emotional intelligence, JOB VISTA offers a fully integrated, personalized, and adaptive system. It aims to bridge the gap between education and employment, empower users with actionable career guidance, and support long-term professional success in fast-evolving technology careers.

Research Objectives

Design a Dual-Perspective Personality Profiling Model

Develop a hybrid model using BERT and Random Forest Classifier to generate personality insights based on self-assessment and verified employer feedback. This enables more accurate, dynamic personality predictions that evolve with user interactions.

Implement Adaptive Learning with Intelligent Quiz Generation

Use retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) techniques to create quizzes that adapt in real time based on learner performance and personality type, allowing a customized learning experience that evolves with user progress.

Integrate Real-Time Emotion Tracking in Interview Preparation

Leverage facial recognition and affective computing to assess emotional cues like confidence, anxiety, or engagement during mock interviews—providing real-time feedback to help users improve soft skills.

Develop Dynamic CV and Career Recommendation Engine

Design an AI-based engine that automatically generates CVs and recommends career paths based on personality traits, learning behavior, and emotional readiness. Job suggestions are matched using semantic similarity and vector-based filtering.

Enable Real-Time Learning & Career Progress Dashboards

Create a real-time dashboard that visualizes user progress, personality alignment, emotional trends, and readiness for job roles—enabling a full-picture view of professional growth and development.

Methodology

Figure 1. High-Level Architecture of the system.

The JOB VISTA system is designed around four core AI-driven components that work together to deliver intelligent personality profiling, adaptive learning, emotional feedback, and personalized career guidance:

1. Dual Personality Profiling Engine: The system uses a BERT and Random Forest Classifier to generate two personality profiles: one based on user self-assessment and another from employer feedback or external evaluation. These profiles evolve over time, enabling dynamic and personalized insights.

2. Adaptive Learning & Intelligent Quiz Module: Using real-time behavioral data and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), this module delivers quizzes and learning content that adapt to the user’s pace, performance, and personality. It ensures a personalized learning journey, classifying users as fast, medium, or slow learners, and tailoring content accordingly for maximum engagement and retention.

3. Emotion-Aware Interview Assistant: Affective computing and emotion recognition via facial analysis and voice tone monitoring are integrated to assess emotional states such as confidence, anxiety, or hesitation during mock interviews. The system provides real-time feedback, enhancing user preparation for professional communication and interview success.

4. Career Recommendation & CV Generation Engine: This module analyzes personality data, learning progress, and emotion metrics to suggest suitable job roles using semantic similarity algorithms (e.g., cosine similarity). It automatically generates CVs tailored to current skills, behaviors, and goals, aligning recommendations with industry trends and user strengths.
